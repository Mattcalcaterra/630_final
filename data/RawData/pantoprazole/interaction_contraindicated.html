  • nelfinavir

    pantoprazole decreases levels of nelfinavir by unspecified interaction mechanism. Contraindicated. Coadministration may lead to loss of nelfinavir virologic response and development of resistance; mechanism may be CYP2C19 inhibition of nelfinavir conversion to active M8 metabolite, and also PPIs decreasing gastric pH resulting in decreased nelfinavir absorption.

  • rilpivirine

    pantoprazole decreases levels of rilpivirine by increasing gastric pH. Applies only to oral form of both agents. Contraindicated. Contraindicated. Concurrent use may cause treatment failure and/or the development of rilpivirine or NNRTI resistance. Coadministration of a PPI and rilpivirine may result in decreased rilpivirine plasma concentrations, which could cause impaired virologic response to rilpivirine.
